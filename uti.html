Get aware about UTI!

UTI

A urinary tract infection (UTI) is an infection that mainly affects the lower part of the urinary system: bladder, urethra and sometimes even the kidneys. Typical symptoms are a lasting need to urinate, together with a burning pain when doing so. However, frequently the affected person can urinate only in small amounts, which eventually becomes very frustrating. Women, who have a higher chance to contract UTIs due to their physiology (closeness of urethra and anus), additionally suffer from pelvic pain. A UTI is a tedious and distressing disease.

It is estimated that 90% of UTIs are caused by E. coli bacteria. These bacteria reside in the bowel tract, amongst others, and are found in feces. Therefore, bad hygiene, sexual activity and the use of certain birth control types are considered risk factors and favor the contraction of a UTI.

Because a UTI can spread to the kidneys, and in some cases become life threatening, it is very commonly treated with antibiotics. However, roughly 25% of women affected by a UTI get the disease again within the following year. This high chance of recurrence and the possibility to develop a chronic UTI makes the administration of antibiotics less effective and is a dangerous strategy, since the patient will develop an antibiotic resistance. Hence alternative treatments for UTI are necessary.

Alternative treatment

Home remedies

Good Hydration

Prevents and treats UTIs. On average a good hydration requires at least 6 to 8 glasses of water each day. This amount of water will remove waste from the body and provide it with important nutrients and electrolytes. Moreover, drinking enough water will dilute the urine and accelerates its journey through the system. This makes it difficult for bacteria to access the cells within the urinary organs making the infection less likely.

Urinating as soon as this is needed

Continuous urination will reduce the amount of bacteria the urine by clearing them out of urinary tract. This will decrease the risk of bacteria accessing and fixing themselves to the urinary tract.

Cranberry juice and capsules

Cranberry has properties that may hinder E.coli cells to attach to cells within the urinary tract. Cranberry Juice is one of the most accepted natural treatments for UTIs. It carries antioxidants including polyphenols which hold antibacterial and anti-inflammatory properties. A recommended amount of 400 ml. with at least 25 percent cranberry juice a day will prevent/treat UTIs. Cranberry capsules/supplements are an alternative for avoiding the high levels of sugar and unpleasant taste of the cranberry juice.

Blueberries

Have similar properties to cranberry, keeping bad bacteria from sticking to the urinary tract organs

Vitamin C

Acidifies urine, hindering the growth of bacteria. Dr. Suzanne Salamon from the Harvard Medical School recommends taking Vitamin C supplements of 500 to 1000 mg on a daily basis or eating foods rich in Vitamin C such as oranges, grapefruits, strawberries, and green vegetables.
